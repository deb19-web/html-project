PROGRAMS

Assess your abilities and categorize your skills according to your level of expertise, from novice to master,
to better understand your strengths and areas for growth.

Home

Levels of skills:

  1. Novice: Just starting out; learning the basics and familiarizing oneself with fundamental concepts and techniques.
  2. Beginner: Gaining some experience; able to perform basic tasks with some degree of competence but may still struggle
    with more complex elements.
  3. Intermediate: More comfortable and proficient; can handle a range of tasks with some skill and problem-solving
    abilities but may need more practice to refine techniques.
  4. Advanced: Highly skilled and capable; can tackle complex tasks with confidence and efficiency; often has a
    deeper understanding of the subject and can apply knowledge flexibly.
  5. Expert: Mastery level; demonstrates exceptional skill and deep understanding; can innovate and solve
    complex problems with ease; often seen as a reference or authority in the field.
  6. Master: Often considered beyond expert; has profound expertise and a high level of creativity and insight;
    may contribute to the development of new techniques or theories and have significant influence in the field.

Novice Home Workout Program

It focuses on building a foundation of strength, endurance, and flexibility. Aim to do this routine 3 times a week, allowing rest days in between.

Warm-Up (5-10 minutes)

  • Jumping Jacks - 1 minute
  • High Knees - 1 minute
  • Arm Circles - 30 seconds each direction
  • Bodyweight Squats - 1 minute

Workout Routine (20-30 minutes)

  1. Bodyweight Squats
    • Reps: 10-15
    • Sets: 2-3
    • Rest: 30-60 seconds between sets
  2. Push-Ups (Modified on knees if needed)
    • Reps: 8-12
    • Sets: 2-3
    • Rest: 30-60 seconds between sets
  3. Plank
    • Duration: 20-30 seconds
    • Sets: 2-3
    • Rest: 30-60 seconds between sets
  4. Lunges
    • Reps: 10-12 per leg
    • Sets: 2-3
    • Rest: 30-60 seconds between sets
  5. Glute Bridges
    • Reps: 15
    • Sets: 2-3
    • Rest: 30-60 seconds between sets

Cool-Down (5-10 minutes)

  1. Standing Forward Bend - 1 minute
  2. Cat-Cow Stretch - 1 minute
  3. Child’s Pose - 1 minute
  4. Seated Hamstring Stretch - 1 minute each leg
Tips:

  • Form Over Reps: Focus on maintaining proper form to prevent injury.

  • Progress Gradually: Increase reps, sets, or duration as you get more comfortable.

  • Hydrate and Rest: Drink plenty of water and ensure adequate recovery time between workouts.

Feel free to modify the exercises based on your comfort level and gradually increase the intensity as you gain strength and confidence.

Beginner Home Workout Program

It’s designed to build strength, improve cardiovascular fitness, and enhance flexibility.

Warm-Up (5-10 minutes)

  1. Arm Circles - 30 seconds forward, 30 seconds backward
  2. Hip Circles - 30 seconds each direction
  3. Leg Swings - 30 seconds each leg (front-to-back)

Workout Routine (20-30 minutes)

  1. Bodyweight Squats
    • Reps: 12-15
    • Sets: 2-3
    • Rest: 30-60 seconds between sets
  2. Push-Ups
    • Reps: 8-12
    • Sets: 2-3
    • Rest: 30-60 seconds between sets
  3. Lunges
    1. Reps: 15-17 per leg
    2. Sets: 2-3
    3. Rest: 30-60 seconds between sets
  4. Plank
    1. Duration: 20-30 seconds
    2. Sets: 2-3
    3. Rest: 30-60 seconds between sets
  5. Glute Bridges
    • Reps: 12-15
    • Sets: 2-3
    • Rest: 30-60 seconds between sets

Cool-Down (5-10 minutes)

  1. Standing Forward Bend - 1 minute
  2. Cat-Cow Stretch - 1 minut3
  3. Seated Hamstring Stretch - 1 minute each leg
Tips:

  • Focus on Form: Ensure proper form for each exercise to maximize benefits and prevent injury.

  • Gradual Progression: Start with the recommended reps and sets, and gradually increase as you become morecomfortable and stronger.

  • Stay Hydrated: Drink water before, during, and after your workout.

  • Listen to Your Body: If you experience pain (not to be confused with muscle fatigue), stop and assess.

This program can be performed 2-3 times a week with rest days in between. Adjust the intensity based on your fitness level and progress as you build strength and endurance.

Intermediate Home Workout Program

This program builds on basic exercises and incorporates more challenging movements to enhance strength, endurance, and flexibility. It requires minimal equipment, such as dumbbells or resistance bands, but can be adapted based on what you have available.

Warm-Up (5-10 minutes)

  1. Jump Rope (or simulate without a rope) - 1 minute
  2. Dynamic Lunges - 1 minute (alternating legs)
  3. Arm Swings and Circles - 1 minute (30 seconds forward, 30 seconds backward)
  4. Leg Swings - 1 minute (30 seconds each leg)

Workout Routine (30-40 minutes)

  1. Goblet Squats
    • Reps: 12-15
    • Sets: 3
    • Rest: 60 seconds between sets
    • Equipment: Dumbbell or kettlebell (hold it at chest level)
  2. Push-Ups
    • Reps: 10-15
    • Sets: 3
    • Rest: 60 seconds between sets
    • Variation: Standard or decline (feet elevated)
  3. Dumbbell or Resistance Band Rows
    • Reps: 12-15
    • Sets: 3
    • Rest: 60 seconds between sets
    • Equipment: Dumbbell or resistance band
  4. Bulgarian Split Squats
    • Reps: 10-12 per leg
    • Sets: 3
    • Rest: 60 seconds between sets
    • Equipment: Elevated surface (like a chair) for rear leg
  5. Plank with Shoulder Taps
    • Reps: 20-30 taps (10-15 each side)
    • Sets: 3
    • Rest: 60 seconds between sets
  6. Dumbbell or Bodyweight Lunges
    • Reps: 12-15 per leg
    • Sets: 3
    • Rest: 60 seconds between sets
    • Equipment: Optional dumbbells
  7. 7. Russian Twists
    • Reps: 20-30 twists (10-15 each side)
    • Sets: 3
    • Rest: 60 seconds between sets
    • Equipment: Dumbbell or weight (optional)
  8. Glute Bridges with Single Leg Lift (for added challenge)
    • o Reps: 10-12 per leg
    • o Sets: 3
    • o Rest: 60 seconds between sets

Cool-Down (5-10 minutes)

  1. Standing Forward Bend - 1 minute
  2. Pigeon Pose - 1 minute per side
  3. Seated Hamstring Stretch - 1 minute per leg
  4. Chest Stretch - 1 minute (hands behind back, gently lift arms)
Tips:

  • Proper Form: Ensure proper technique to avoid injury and maximize effectiveness.

  • Intensity: Adjust weights and reps as needed based on your fitness level and goals.

  • Progression: Increase weights, reps, or sets as you get stronger.

  • Recovery: Allow adequate rest and recovery between workouts to avoid overtraining.

This program can be done 3-4 times a week. Incorporate rest days or lighter activity (like walking or yoga) to allow your body to recover.

Advanced Home Workout Program

This program incorporates high-intensity exercises and complex movements to challenge strength, endurance, and flexibility. It’s designed for those who have a solid foundation and are looking to push their limits.

Warm-Up (10 minutes)

  1. High Knees - 1 minute
  2. Burpees - 1 minute
  3. Mountain Climbers - 1 minute
  4. Dynamic Lunges - 1 minute
  5. Arm Circles and Swings - 1 minute (30 seconds each direction)
  6. Leg Swings - 1 minute (30 seconds each leg)

Workout Routine (40-50 minutes)

  1. Front Squats with Dumbbells or Kettlebell
    • Reps: 12-15
    • Sets: 4
    • Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets
    • Equipment: Dumbbells or kettlebell (hold at shoulder level)
  2. Explosive Push-Ups
    • Reps: 10-12
    • Sets: 4
    • Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets
    • Variation: Push up with a clap or explosive push up from the ground
  3. Single-Leg Deadlifts
    • Reps: 10-12 per leg
    • Sets: 4
    • Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets
    • Equipment: Dumbbell or kettlebell
  4. Bulgarian Split Squats with Dumbbells
    • Reps: 12-15 per leg
    • Sets: 4
    • Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets
    • Equipment: Dumbbells or kettlebell
  5. Pistol Squats (to a box or chair if needed for balance)
    • Reps: 8-10 per leg
    • Sets: 3
    • Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets
  6. Renegade Rows
    • Reps: 12-15 per side
    • Sets: 4
    • Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets
    • Equipment: Dumbbells
  7. Plank to Push-Up
    • Reps: 10-15
    • Sets: 4
    • Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets
  8. Hanging Leg Raises or Lying Leg Raises
    • Reps: 15-20
    • Sets: 4
    • Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets
    • Equipment: Pull-up bar or lying on the floor
  9. Russian Twists with Medicine Ball or Dumbbell
    • Reps: 20-30 twists (10-15 each side)
    • Sets: 4
    • Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets
  10. Burpee Box Jumps
    • Reps: 10-15
    • Sets: 3
    • Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets
    • Equipment: Stable platform or box

Cool-Down (10 minutes)

  1. Standing Forward Bend - 1 minute
  2. Pigeon Pose - 1 minute per side
  3. Seated Hamstring Stretch - 1 minute per leg
  4. Cobra Stretch - 1 minute
  5. Child’s Pose - 1 minute
  6. Spinal Twist - 1 minute per side
Tips:

  • Intensity: Adjust weights and exercise difficulty to match your fitness level. Ensure you’re pushing yourself but not compromising form.

  • Form and Safety: Proper technique is crucial to prevent injury. Consider using a mirror or recording yourself to check form.

  • Progressive Overload: Gradually increase weights, reps, or sets as you become stronger.

  • Recovery: Allow at least one rest day between intense workouts to aid recovery. Incorporate light activities or stretching on rest days.

This program can be performed 4-5 times a week, with adequate rest and recovery. Adjust the volume or intensity as needed to suit your individual fitness goals and conditions.

Expert Home Workout Program

An expert-level home workout program will focus on advanced strength, endurance, and agility training. It incorporates high-intensity intervals, complex movements, and challenging exercises to push your limits. This program assumes you have a high level of fitness and experience with a variety of exercises. It also requires some equipment like dumbbells, a pull-up bar, and possibly resistance bands.

Warm-Up (10-15 minutes)

  1. Jump Rope - 2 minutes
  2. Burpees - 1 minute
  3. High Knees - 1 minute
  4. Dynamic Lunges - 1 minute
  5. Arm Circles and Swings - 1 minute (30 seconds each direction)
  6. Leg Swings - 1 minute (30 seconds each leg)
  7. Hip Circles - 1 minute

Workout Routine (50-60 minutes)

  1. Barbell or Dumbbell Deadlifts (If you don’t have a barbell, use heavy dumbbells)
    • Reps: 10-12
    • Sets: 4
    • Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets
  2. Explosive Plyometric Push-Ups
    • Reps: 10-15
    • Sets: 4
    • Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets
  3. Single-Leg Romanian Deadlifts with Dumbbells
    • Reps: 10-12 per leg
    • Sets: 4
    • Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets
  4. Pull-Ups or Chin-Ups
    • Reps: 8-12
    • Sets: 4
    • Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets
    • Variation: Weighted if possible
  5. Pistol Squats
    • Reps: 8-12 per leg
    • Sets: 4
    • Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets
  6. Renegade Rows with Dumbbells
    • Reps: 12-15 per side
    • Sets: 4
    • Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets
  7. Hanging Leg Raises
    • Reps: 15-20
    • Sets: 4
    • Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets
  8. Kettlebell Swings
    • Reps: 15-20
    • Sets: 4
    • Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets
  9. Box Jumps or Broad Jumps
    • Reps: 10-15
    • Sets: 4
    • Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets
  10. Barbell or Dumbbell Thrusters
    • Reps: 10-12
    • Sets: 4
    • Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets
  11. Mountain Climbers (High Speed)
    • Duration: 1 minute
    • Sets: 3
    • Rest: 60 seconds between sets

Cool-Down (10-15 minutes)

  1. Standing Forward Bend - 1 minute
  2. Seated Hamstring Stretch - 1 minute per leg
  3. Spinal Twist - 1 minute per side
  4. Child’s Pose - 1 minute
  5. Cobra Stretch - 1 minute
Tips:

  • Intensity and Variation: Adjust weights, reps, and rest periods to match your individual fitness level and goals. Incorporate different variations to continuously challenge yourself.

  • Form and Technique: Prioritize proper form to prevent injury and maximize effectiveness.

  • Progressive Overload: Gradually increase the intensity of your workouts by adding weight, reps, or sets as you progress.

  • Recovery: Allow for adequate rest and recovery between workouts. Incorporate active recovery and flexibility work on off days.

This advanced program is designed to be intense and challenging, so listen to your body and make adjustments as needed. Aim to complete this routine 4-5 times a week, with at least one full rest day or light recovery activity.

Master-Level Home Workout Program

A master-level home workout program is designed for individuals who have achieved a high level of fitness and are looking to maintain or further enhance their peak physical condition. This program includes highly advanced exercises, incorporates multiple training modalities, and focuses on strength, power, endurance, and agility. It also assumes access to a range of equipment and a deep understanding of proper technique.

Warm-Up (15 minutes)

  1. Dynamic Warm-Up Routine:
    • Jump Rope - 3 minutes
    • High Knees - 1 minute
    • Butt Kicks - 1 minute
    • Arm Circles - 1 minute (30 seconds each direction)
    • Leg Swings - 1 minute (30 seconds each leg)
    • Hip Circles - 1 minute (30 seconds each direction)
    • Spiderman Stretch - 1 minute (30 seconds each side)
  2. Mobility Drills:
    • Deep Squat Hold - 1 minute
    • World’s Greatest Stretch - 1 minute (30 seconds each side)
    • Couch Stretch - 1 minute (30 seconds each side)

Workout Routine (60-75 minutes)

  1. Barbell Squats or Front Squats
    • Reps: 8-12
    • Sets: 4-5
    • Rest: 90-120 seconds between sets
  2. Deadlifts (Conventional or Romanian)
    • Reps: 8-12
    • Sets: 4-5
    • Rest: 90-120 seconds between sets
  3. Weighted Pull-Ups or Muscle-Ups
    • Reps: 6-10
    • Sets: 4
    • Rest: 90-120 seconds between sets
    • Variation: Weighted or adding muscle-ups for extra challenge
  4. Barbell or Dumbbell Bench Press
    • Reps: 8-12
    • Sets: 4-5
    • Rest: 90-120 seconds between sets
  5. Kettlebell Swings or Snatches
    • Reps: 15-20
    • Sets: 4
    • Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets
  6. Pistol Squats with Weight
    • Reps: 6-10 per leg
    • Sets: 4
    • Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets
  7. Barbell Clean and Press or Dumbbell Complex
    • Reps: 8-10
    • Sets: 4
    • Rest: 90-120 seconds between sets
  8. Hanging Leg Raises or Toes-to-Bar
    • Reps: 15-20
    • Sets: 4
    • Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets
  9. Box Jumps or Broad Jumps
    • Reps: 10-15
    • Sets: 4
    • Rest: 60-90 seconds between sets
  10. Battle Ropes or High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT)
    • o Duration: 30-45 seconds work, 15-30 seconds rest
    • o Sets: 5-6 rounds

Cool-Down (10-15 minutes)

  1. Static Stretching:
    • Standing Forward Bend - 1 minute
    • Pigeon Pose - 1 minute per side
    • Seated Hamstring Stretch - 1 minute per leg
    • Cobra Stretch - 1 minute
    • Child’s Pose - 1 minute
  2. Foam Rolling (Optional but recommended):
    • Quads - 1 minute per side
    • Hamstrings - 1 minute per side
    • Back - 1 minute
    • Glutes - 1 minute per side
Tips:

  • Technical Mastery: Ensure you maintain impeccable form to maximize benefits and avoid injury. Advanced exercises often require precise technique.

  • Progressive Overload: Continually challenge yourself by increasing weights, adjusting exercise variations, or adding more complex movements.

  • Recovery: Prioritize recovery and consider integrating techniques like active recovery, deep tissue massage, or additional mobility work on rest days.

  • Periodization: To avoid plateaus and overtraining, consider varying your workout routine periodically or integrating different training phases (e.g., strength, hypertrophy, power).

This master-level program is designed to push your limits, so listen to your body and adjust as necessary. Aim for 4-6 workouts per week, with sufficient rest and recovery to support optimal performance and progress.
